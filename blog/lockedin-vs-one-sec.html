LOCKEDIN vs One Sec: Friction vs Force — Which Actually Reduces Screen Time?

Updated March 2026 · 7 min read

One Sec is one of the most popular screen time apps on iOS. It uses a clever approach: before opening a distracting app, it makes you take a deep breath and wait a few seconds. The idea is that this moment of friction breaks the automatic habit loop.

LOCKEDIN takes a completely different approach: it blocks your apps entirely until you exercise.

These represent two fundamentally different philosophies about behavior change. Let's break down which one actually works.

The Philosophy: Friction vs Force

One Sec believes in friction — if you make a bad habit slightly harder, you'll do it less. The breathing exercise creates a pause that lets your prefrontal cortex override the dopamine-seeking impulse.

LOCKEDIN believes in force — remove the option entirely and replace it with something better. You physically cannot access your apps until you've exercised. No pause, no decision point, no willpower needed.

The Fundamental Problem with Friction

One Sec's friction approach works — initially. Studies show it reduces app opens by about 57% in the first weeks. That's impressive.

But here's the problem: your brain adapts to friction.

After a few weeks, the breathing exercise becomes automatic. You breathe, you wait, you open the app anyway. The pause that once interrupted your habit loop becomes part of it. This is called habituation — your nervous system stops responding to a repeated stimulus.

LOCKEDIN doesn't have this problem because there's nothing to habituate to. The apps are blocked. Period. The only variable is how much you've exercised today.

The Double Win: Less Screen Time + More Exercise

One Sec reduces screen time (sometimes). That's its entire value proposition.

LOCKEDIN reduces screen time and increases exercise. It's a two-for-one deal:

  • Your screen time drops because apps are actually blocked
  • Your exercise increases because it's the only way to unlock them
  • You feel better because exercise produces real endorphins (not just a breathing pause)
  • You sleep better because both less screen time AND more exercise improve sleep
