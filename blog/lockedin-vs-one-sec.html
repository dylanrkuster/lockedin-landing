LOCKEDIN vs One Sec: Which Approach to Phone Addiction Actually Works?

Updated March 2026 · 7 min read

One Sec is one of the most popular screen time apps on iOS right now. The concept is clever: before you can open a distracting app, it makes you take a deep breath and wait a few seconds. That little pause is supposed to interrupt the autopilot habit loop and give your brain a chance to reconsider.

LOCKEDIN does something completely different. It treats your screen time like a bank account. You earn minutes by exercising. You spend them by using apps. Run out of minutes and your apps get blocked until you move again.

These are two genuinely different philosophies about how to change behavior. Let's break down how each one works in practice.

Friction vs Currency

One Sec adds friction. It believes that if you make a bad habit slightly harder to do, you'll do it less often. The breathing exercise creates a small speed bump between your impulse and the action.

LOCKEDIN creates a currency. Your screen time has a real, tangible cost. Every minute you spend scrolling is a minute you earned through exercise. It turns phone usage from something you do on autopilot into something you actively budget.

Think about the difference. One Sec asks "are you sure?" LOCKEDIN asks "how much of your balance do you want to spend on this?"

That second question is way more powerful. It's the same reason you think twice about buying something when you check your bank account first, but you wouldn't think twice if someone just said "hey, are you sure you want to buy that?"

The Habituation Problem

One Sec works at first. Their own data says it reduces app opens by about 57% in the first few weeks. That's genuinely impressive.

But here's the thing: your brain gets used to it.

After a few weeks of the breathing exercise, it stops being a meaningful interruption. You breathe, you wait, you open the app anyway. The pause becomes part of the routine instead of breaking it. Psychologists call this habituation. Your nervous system just stops caring about a repeated stimulus.

LOCKEDIN doesn't have this problem. There's nothing to get used to. Your balance is either above zero or it isn't. The math doesn't care how many times you've seen it before.

The Part Nobody Talks About: Guilt

This is the thing that really sets LOCKEDIN apart, and almost nobody mentions it.

With every other screen time app, using your phone feels like failing. You bypassed the block, you tapped through the warning, you ignored the limit. There's always this low-grade guilt in the background.

LOCKEDIN eliminates that entirely.

When you use apps with LOCKEDIN, you're spending minutes you earned. You ran 3 miles this morning. You walked 8,000 steps. That screen time is yours. You worked for it. There is zero reason to feel bad about watching YouTube for 30 minutes when you literally earned those 30 minutes with your body.

This sounds like a small thing. It's actually everything. It's the difference between a diet that makes you feel guilty every time you eat and a nutrition plan where you earn treats through exercise. One of those is sustainable. The other isn't.
