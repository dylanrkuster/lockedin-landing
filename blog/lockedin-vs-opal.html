LOCKEDIN vs Opal: Which Screen Time App Actually Works in 2026?

Updated March 2026 · 8 min read

So you want to use your phone less. You've tried the built-in Screen Time limits. You tapped "Ignore" without even thinking about it. Now you're looking at apps that might actually do something.

Opal and LOCKEDIN both want to help you use your phone less. But they work in completely different ways. And that difference matters way more than you'd think.

The Big Difference

Opal blocks apps during scheduled focus sessions. You pick times (like "no social media 9 to 5"), and Opal locks those apps down during that window. Think of it like setting an alarm clock for your willpower.

LOCKEDIN works like a bank account for your screen time. Workouts and steps deposit minutes into your balance. Using apps withdraws from it. When your balance hits zero, your apps get blocked at the system level until you exercise again.

That might sound like a small difference. It's not. It completely changes how you think about your phone.

Why the "Bank Account" Thing Matters

Here's what happens when you use Opal: you set a focus session, your apps get blocked for a few hours, and then the session ends and you're right back to where you started. Nothing changed about your relationship with your phone. You just couldn't access it for a while.

Here's what happens when you use LOCKEDIN: you open Instagram, see your balance dropping in real time, and you instinctively think "is this worth my minutes?"

That question changes everything.

You stop mindless scrolling. Not because the app blocked you. Because you're watching a number go down and your brain treats it like watching money leave your account. You become intentional about every app you open.

And here's the part nobody expects: when you DO use your phone, you don't feel guilty about it. Because you earned those minutes. You ran, you lifted, you walked 10,000 steps. That screen time is yours. You worked for it.

No other screen time app gives you that feeling.

The Psychology Problem with Focus Sessions

Opal's approach has one big flaw: you can always end the session early.

When the craving hits hard enough, you tap through it. And then you feel bad about it. And then you set another session. And then you break that one too. It's a cycle.

LOCKEDIN doesn't have an "end session" button. There's no override. The only way to get more screen time is to move your body. That's not a punishment. It's actually the opposite. You're replacing one dopamine source (your phone) with a better one (exercise). Your brain still gets what it wants. It just gets it from something that's actually good for you.

FAQ

Can I use both Opal and LOCKEDIN?

Technically yes, but there's no reason to. Both use Apple's FamilyControls, and running two blocking apps at once can cause conflicts. Pick the approach that makes more sense for you and commit to it.

Does LOCKEDIN work without an Apple Watch?

Absolutely. You can log workouts directly in Apple Health, and LOCKEDIN also counts steps from your iPhone's built-in pedometer. You don't need any wearable.

Is Opal worth $100/year?

For teams and companies that need structured focus time, maybe. For someone trying to use their phone less as an individual, LOCKEDIN does more for a tenth of the price.
